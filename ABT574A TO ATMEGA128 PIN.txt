Key Components & Configuration
5x7 Dot Matrix Display (Common Anode): Typically features 5 columns (cathodes) and 7 rows (anodes). A common configuration is "anode row, cathode column".
SN74ABT574A Flip-Flops: These 8-bit, positive-edge-triggered D-type flip-flops feature 3-state outputs. They are used to hold the data for the 5 columns while the display scans through the rows.
Multiplexing Strategy: Because the display is common anode, the rows are selected one by one (active high) while the 5 columns are driven low (active low) to turn on specific LEDs in that row.
